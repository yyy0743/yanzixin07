Healthcare Facilities Coverage in Singapore

Healthcare Facilities Coverage in Singapore is a data visualization project that examines the distribution of healthcare facilities across HDB residential areas.

The storymap leverages government open data to map the locations of hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies across Singapore. To assess coverage, the project defines service areas based on network distances: hospitals are considered accessible within 1,000 meters, while clinics and pharmacies are evaluated within a 500-meter range. This analysis helps identify well-served areas as well as potential gaps in healthcare coverage.

Through the crowdsourced map, users can contribute by reporting areas where healthcare services may be lacking or sharing insights on alternative medical service points via the Ushahidi platform.
